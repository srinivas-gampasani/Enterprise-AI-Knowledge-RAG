PAID TIME OFF (PTO) POLICY
================================================================================

ACCRUAL RATES (Full-Time Employees):
Years of Service  |  Hours Accrued per Pay Period  |  Annual Total
0–2 years         |  4.62 hours                    |  120 hours (15 days)
3–5 years         |  5.54 hours                    |  144 hours (18 days)
6–10 years        |  6.46 hours                    |  168 hours (21 days)
11–15 years       |  7.38 hours                    |  192 hours (24 days)
16+ years         |  8.31 hours                    |  216 hours (27 days)

Part-time (20–32 hrs/week): Accrues at 50% of full-time rate
Per diem / PRN: Not eligible for PTO accrual

PTO CASH-OUT:
- Employees may cash out up to 40 hours of accrued PTO once per calendar year
- Request submitted through Workday by December 1 for December 15 payout
- Maximum PTO balance: 400 hours (accrual stops at cap)

LEAVES OF ABSENCE:
- FMLA: Up to 12 weeks unpaid per year (run concurrent with PTO if applicable)
- Parental Leave: 6 weeks paid (birth, adoption, foster placement)
- Bereavement: 3 days (immediate family); 1 day (extended family)
- Military Leave: Per USERRA; difference in pay maintained for first 30 days

CREDENTIALING & PRIVILEGING POLICY
================================================================================

POLICY ID: AVC-MED-STAFF-007 | Effective: September 2024

INITIAL CREDENTIALING PROCESS:
Required Documents for All Applicants:
1. Completed AVC Medical Staff Application (online via symplr system)
2. Current state medical license (verified via NLMS primary source)
3. DEA certificate with Kansas / Missouri endorsement as applicable
4. Board certification (required within 5 years of completing residency)
5. Current BLS + ACLS certificates (PALS for pediatric privileges)
6. Malpractice coverage verification (minimum $1M/$3M; tail required)
7. Training certificates / diplomas (primary source verified)
8. NPDB query results (self-query + AVC-initiated query)
9. 5-year work history with explanation of all gaps > 30 days
10. 3 peer references (must include current department chair)

TIMELINE:
- Application receipt to credentials committee review: 30 days
- Credentials committee to Medical Executive Committee: 14 days
- MEC to Board approval: 14 days
- Total target: < 60 days from completed application

ONGOING RE-CREDENTIALING: Every 2 years
Criteria reviewed: Case volume, peer review flags, patient complaints, CME compliance,
malpractice history, license and board certification currency

WORKPLACE VIOLENCE PREVENTION POLICY
================================================================================

POLICY ID: AVC-HR-018 | Effective: June 2024

DEFINITION:
Workplace violence includes physical assaults, threats, verbal abuse, stalking, or any behavior
that creates a reasonable fear of harm in the workplace.

REPORTING REQUIREMENTS:
- Any incident of workplace violence MUST be reported immediately
- Report to: Direct supervisor AND Security (ext. 5911) AND Risk Management
- Document in Quantros Safety Event Reporting System within 24 hours
- Law enforcement contacted for any physical assault or credible threat

DE-ESCALATION TRAINING:
- All patient-facing staff: Mandatory annual CPI (Crisis Prevention Intervention) training
- ED, Behavioral Health, and ICU staff: Enhanced 8-hour CPI re-certification annually
- Security team: Monthly scenario-based training

PANIC BUTTON PROTOCOL:
- Duress buttons located at every nursing station and registration desk
- Press triggers: Security response (< 3 min), overhead "Staff Assist" announcement
- Personal duress devices available for check-out from Security for high-risk patient assignments

VISITOR MANAGEMENT:
- All visitors screened at main entrances 24/7
- Visitor badges required and color-coded by unit and date
- Behavioral Health and Oncology: Visitor restrictions enforced by Security
- No-visitor orders: Document in EMR and notify Security and Reception

PERFORMANCE REVIEW PROCESS
================================================================================

ANNUAL REVIEW CYCLE:
- Self-assessment completed by employee: April 1
- Manager assessment completed: April 15
- Calibration meetings with HR Business Partner: April 22
- Review conversations completed: May 1
- Merit increases effective: June 1

PERFORMANCE RATINGS:
5 — Exceptional: Consistently exceeds all expectations; top 10% of peers
4 — Exceeds Expectations: Regularly surpasses most goals
3 — Meets Expectations: Fully competent; achieves all core goals
2 — Development Needed: Partially meets expectations; improvement plan required
1 — Does Not Meet Expectations: Fails to meet core job requirements; formal PIP initiated

MERIT INCREASE GUIDELINES (FY2025):
Rating 5: 5.0–6.0% increase
Rating 4: 3.5–4.5% increase
Rating 3: 2.0–3.0% increase
Rating 2: 0–1.0% increase
Rating 1: 0% increase (ineligible for merit)

PROMOTION POLICY:
- Internal posting required for minimum 5 business days before external posting
- Internal candidates meeting minimum qualifications guaranteed an interview
- Promotion increases: 8–12% above current salary (or to band minimum, whichever is greater)

HIPAA COMPLIANCE & DATA PRIVACY POLICY
================================================================================

POLICY ID: AVC-HIPAA-001 | Effective: January 2025 | Annual Review Required

PROTECTED HEALTH INFORMATION (PHI):
PHI includes any individually identifiable health information in any format (electronic, paper, oral)
including: name, address, DOB, phone, SSN, medical record number, account numbers, photos,
diagnosis, treatment, payment information.

MINIMUM NECESSARY STANDARD:
- Access only the PHI necessary to perform your job function
- Do not access records of family members, coworkers, public figures, or yourself
- All access is logged and audited; violations investigated within 5 business days

PERMITTED USES WITHOUT PATIENT AUTHORIZATION:
- Treatment: Care team members involved in patient's care
- Payment: Billing and insurance functions
- Healthcare Operations: Quality improvement, staff training, accreditation

BREACH NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS:
- Workforce member discovering potential breach: Report to Privacy Officer within 1 hour
- Privacy Officer risk assessment: Completed within 24 hours
- If breach confirmed: Affected individuals notified within 60 days
- HHS OCR notification: Within 60 days (or annual report if < 500 individuals)
- Media notification: Required if > 500 residents in a state affected

SANCTIONS:
Tier 1 (unintentional, no PHI exposure): Verbal warning + retraining
Tier 2 (unintentional, limited PHI exposure): Written warning + retraining + monitoring
Tier 3 (willful neglect / repeated): Suspension or termination + law enforcement referral
Tier 4 (malicious access / sale of PHI): Immediate termination + criminal referral
