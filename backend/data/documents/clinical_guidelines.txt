SEPSIS MANAGEMENT PROTOCOL
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Sepsis is a life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection.
Septic shock is a subset of sepsis with circulatory and cellular/metabolic dysfunction associated
with a higher risk of mortality.

SCREENING CRITERIA (SIRS-based + qSOFA):
- Temperature > 38.3°C or < 36°C
- Heart Rate > 90 bpm
- Respiratory Rate > 20 breaths/min or PaCO2 < 32 mmHg
- WBC > 12,000 or < 4,000 cells/µL or > 10% bands
- Altered mental status (GCS drop ≥ 2 points)
- Systolic BP < 100 mmHg

SEPSIS 3-HOUR BUNDLE (Must be completed within 3 hours of presentation):
1. Measure lactate level — target < 2 mmol/L; if > 4 mmol/L, initiate shock protocol
2. Obtain blood cultures BEFORE initiating antibiotics (minimum 2 sets)
3. Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics (see Antibiotic Selection Guide below)
4. Administer 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate ≥ 4 mmol/L

ANTIBIOTIC SELECTION:
- Community-acquired: Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV q8h + Vancomycin 25mg/kg IV
- Hospital-acquired / VAP: Meropenem 1g IV q8h + Vancomycin
- Immunocompromised: Add Micafungin 100mg IV daily for fungal coverage
- Penicillin allergy: Aztreonam 2g IV q8h + Metronidazole 500mg IV q8h

VASOPRESSOR THRESHOLD:
- Initiate Norepinephrine if MAP < 65 mmHg despite adequate fluid resuscitation
- Target MAP ≥ 65 mmHg
- Add Vasopressin 0.03 units/min if Norepinephrine dose > 0.25 mcg/kg/min
- Hydrocortisone 200mg/day IV infusion for refractory septic shock

MONITORING PARAMETERS:
- Continuous pulse oximetry and cardiac monitoring
- Lactate reassessment at 2 hours; repeat q6h until < 2 mmol/L
- Urine output goal: > 0.5 mL/kg/hr
- CVP monitoring if central line placed: target 8–12 mmHg

ACUTE MYOCARDIAL INFARCTION (STEMI) PROTOCOL
================================================================================

DOOR-TO-BALLOON TIME TARGET: < 90 minutes

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS ON STEMI DIAGNOSIS:
1. Activate Cath Lab via overhead page: "STEMI Alert, Cath Lab 1"
2. Administer Aspirin 325mg PO (chew and swallow) immediately
3. Administer Ticagrelor 180mg PO loading dose (preferred over Clopidogrel)
4. Anticoagulation: Heparin 60 units/kg IV bolus (max 4,000 units) + 12 units/kg/hr infusion
5. Obtain 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes of arrival
6. Place large-bore IVs (x2), draw STAT labs: Troponin I, CMP, CBC, PT/INR, Type & Screen
7. Supplemental O2 only if SpO2 < 90%

CONTRAINDICATIONS TO FIBRINOLYSIS (if PCI unavailable):
- Prior intracranial hemorrhage at any time
- Ischemic stroke within 3 months
- Active internal bleeding
- Suspected aortic dissection
- Significant closed-head trauma within 3 months

POST-PCI CARE ORDERS:
- Continue Aspirin 81mg PO daily indefinitely
- Continue Ticagrelor 90mg PO BID for 12 months
- Beta-blocker: Metoprolol succinate 25mg PO daily (titrate to HR 55–65)
- ACE Inhibitor: Lisinopril 5mg PO daily (titrate to 40mg as tolerated)
- Statin: Atorvastatin 80mg PO daily (high-intensity)
- Cardiac Rehabilitation referral within 72 hours of discharge

FALL PREVENTION PROTOCOL
================================================================================

MORSE FALL SCALE SCORING:
- History of falls (last 3 months): No=0, Yes=25
- Secondary diagnosis: No=0, Yes=15
- Ambulatory aid: None/bedrest=0, Crutches/cane=15, Furniture=30
- IV or IV access: No=0, Yes=20
- Gait: Normal/bedrest=0, Weak=10, Impaired=20
- Mental status: Oriented=0, Forgetful/confused=15

RISK CLASSIFICATION:
- Low Risk: 0–24 → Standard safety measures
- Moderate Risk: 25–44 → Implement yellow armband, yellow socks, fall risk sign
- High Risk: ≥ 45 → All above + bed alarm, hourly rounding, move closer to nurses station

INTERVENTIONS FOR HIGH-RISK PATIENTS:
1. Yellow "FALL RISK" wristband applied immediately
2. Bed alarm activated at all times
3. Call light within reach; verify patient knows how to use
4. Non-skid footwear provided
5. Clear path to bathroom; night light activated
6. Hourly safety rounding documented in EMR
7. Notify physician if new fall or near-fall event
8. Sitter order placed if patient is confused and ambulatory

MEDICATION RECONCILIATION POLICY
================================================================================

POLICY ID: AVC-MED-022 | Effective: March 2024

DEFINITION:
Medication reconciliation is the process of comparing a patient's medication orders to all
medications the patient has been taking to avoid medication errors.

REQUIRED RECONCILIATION POINTS:
1. ADMISSION: Complete med rec within 4 hours of patient placement in unit
2. TRANSFER: Prior to transfer between units; receiving nurse verifies within 1 hour
3. DISCHARGE: Physician completes discharge med rec; pharmacist review required for patients
   on ≥ 5 chronic medications or any high-alert medications

HIGH-ALERT MEDICATIONS (Require double-check by two licensed nurses):
- Insulin (all types and concentrations)
- Heparin IV infusions
- Concentrated electrolytes (KCl > 20 mEq, NaCl 3%, MgSO4 50%)
- Chemotherapy agents
- Neuromuscular blocking agents
- Opioids: IV PCA, epidural infusions

DOCUMENTATION IN EPIC:
- Navigate to: Patient Chart → Medications → Reconciliation Workflow
- Mark each home medication as: Continue / Hold / Discontinue / Changed
- Add reason for any medication held or changed
- Pharmacist must co-sign for high-alert medication changes

HAND HYGIENE COMPLIANCE POLICY
================================================================================

POLICY ID: AVC-IC-005 | Effective: January 2025

THE 5 MOMENTS FOR HAND HYGIENE (WHO Standard):
1. Before patient contact
2. Before aseptic task
3. After body fluid exposure risk
4. After patient contact
5. After contact with patient surroundings

TECHNIQUE:
- Alcohol-based hand rub (preferred): Apply 3mL, rub all surfaces for minimum 20 seconds
- Soap and water (required for C. diff, norovirus, visible soiling): 40–60 seconds, rinse, dry
- Jewelry: No rings, bracelets, or artificial nails permitted in patient care areas

MONITORING:
- Secret observer audits: minimum 50 observations per unit per month
- Target compliance: ≥ 95% by unit
- Results reported monthly at Unit Safety Huddle
- Individual coaching for observed non-compliance (anonymous unless repeated)
