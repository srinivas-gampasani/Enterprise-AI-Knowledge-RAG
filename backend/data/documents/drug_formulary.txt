================================================================================
ANTICOAGULATION MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

HEPARIN INFUSION PROTOCOL (Weight-Based):
Initial Bolus: 80 units/kg IV (max 10,000 units)
Initial Rate: 18 units/kg/hr (max 1,600 units/hr)

aPTT ADJUSTMENT SCALE:
- aPTT < 40 sec: Bolus 80 units/kg; increase rate by 4 units/kg/hr
- aPTT 40–49 sec: Bolus 40 units/kg; increase rate by 2 units/kg/hr
- aPTT 50–70 sec (THERAPEUTIC): No change
- aPTT 71–90 sec: Decrease rate by 2 units/kg/hr
- aPTT 91–120 sec: Hold 30 min; decrease rate by 3 units/kg/hr
- aPTT > 120 sec: Hold 60 min; decrease rate by 4 units/kg/hr; notify provider

MONITORING: aPTT every 6 hours until 2 consecutive therapeutic levels, then q12h
Platelet count every 2 days (monitor for HIT — drop > 50% from baseline)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WARFARIN DOSING GUIDANCE:
- Standard initiation: 5mg PO daily for most patients
- Reduced initiation: 2.5mg for age > 75, weight < 50kg, hepatic disease, CYP2C9 variants
- INR monitoring: Daily while in hospital; weekly once stable outpatient
- Target INR ranges:
  * Atrial fibrillation / DVT / PE: 2.0–3.0
  * Mechanical aortic valve: 2.0–3.0
  * Mechanical mitral valve: 2.5–3.5
  * Antiphospholipid syndrome: 2.5–3.5

WARFARIN REVERSAL:
- INR 4.5–10, no bleeding: Hold warfarin; supplement Vitamin K 1–2.5mg PO
- INR > 10, no bleeding: Hold warfarin; Vitamin K 2.5–5mg PO; recheck 24h
- Any INR, serious bleeding: 4-Factor PCC (Kcentra) + Vitamin K 10mg IV slow push

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DIRECT ORAL ANTICOAGULANTS (DOACs):

Apixaban (Eliquis):
- AF stroke prevention: 5mg PO BID (2.5mg BID if ≥ 2 of: age ≥ 80, weight ≤ 60kg, Cr ≥ 1.5)
- VTE treatment: 10mg PO BID x7 days, then 5mg PO BID
- Reversal agent: Andexanet alfa (Andexxa) — 400mg or 800mg IV bolus + infusion

Rivaroxaban (Xarelto):
- AF stroke prevention: 20mg PO daily with evening meal (15mg if CrCl 15–50)
- VTE treatment: 15mg PO BID x21 days with meals, then 20mg PO daily
- Reversal agent: Andexanet alfa

Dabigatran (Pradaxa):
- AF stroke prevention: 150mg PO BID (75mg BID if CrCl 15–30)
- Reversal agent: Idarucizumab (Praxbind) 5g IV (two 2.5g vials)

================================================================================
PAIN MANAGEMENT LADDER
================================================================================

STEP 1 — MILD PAIN (NRS 1–3):
- Acetaminophen 650–1000mg PO/IV q6h (max 4g/day; 2g/day hepatic disease)
- Ibuprofen 400–600mg PO q6h with food (avoid if GFR < 30, GI bleed history)
- Ketorolac 15–30mg IV/IM q6h (max 5 days; avoid if GFR < 30)

STEP 2 — MODERATE PAIN (NRS 4–6):
- Tramadol 50–100mg PO q6h (max 400mg/day; avoid with SSRI — serotonin syndrome risk)
- Hydrocodone/acetaminophen 5mg/325mg PO q4–6h PRN (document PDMP check)
- Oxycodone 5mg PO q4–6h PRN (document PDMP check)

STEP 3 — SEVERE PAIN (NRS 7–10):
- Morphine 2–4mg IV q3–4h PRN; PCA: 1–2mg demand, 6–10 min lockout
- Hydromorphone 0.2–0.4mg IV q3–4h PRN; PCA: 0.1–0.2mg demand, 8 min lockout
- Fentanyl 25–50 mcg IV q1h PRN (preferred for renal failure)

OPIOID SAFETY REQUIREMENTS:
- Naloxone must be prescribed and bedside for any patient on IV opioids
- Naloxone dose: 0.4mg IV/IM/IN; may repeat q2–3 min x3; infusion at 2/3 effective dose/hr
- Respiratory rate < 8 or SpO2 < 90% on opioids: administer naloxone, call rapid response

================================================================================
INSULIN MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

INPATIENT GLYCEMIC TARGETS:
- ICU patients: 140–180 mg/dL
- Non-ICU patients: 140–180 mg/dL (individualized; < 140 if surgical, < 200 if palliative)
- Hypoglycemia threshold: < 70 mg/dL — treat immediately

INSULIN INFUSION PROTOCOL (ICU only):
Starting rate = Blood glucose / 100 (units/hr, rounded to nearest 0.5)
- BG 140–180: No change
- BG 181–220: Increase rate by 0.5 units/hr
- BG > 220: Increase rate by 1 unit/hr
- BG 70–109: Decrease rate by 50%; recheck in 30 min
- BG < 70: Hold infusion; administer D50W 25mL IV; recheck in 15 min; notify MD

SUBCUTANEOUS INSULIN — BASAL/BOLUS ORDERS:
- Calculate Total Daily Dose (TDD): 0.4–0.5 units/kg/day for insulin-naive
- Basal: 50% of TDD as Glargine (Lantus) or Detemir at bedtime
- Nutritional: 50% of TDD divided as Aspart (NovoLog) with each meal
- Correction scale: per pharmacy sliding scale based on TDD

HIGH-ALERT INSULIN REMINDERS:
- Two-nurse double-check required for all insulin drip rate changes
- U-500 insulin: Order must state "U-500" explicitly; stored separately in Pyxis
- Never abbreviate "Units" as "U" (mistaken as zero — use "units" in full)

================================================================================
CONTRAST MEDIA PREMEDICATION PROTOCOL
================================================================================

For patients with prior contrast reaction or high risk:
- Methylprednisolone 32mg PO at 12h and 2h before procedure, OR
- Prednisone 50mg PO at 13h, 7h, and 1h before procedure
- PLUS Diphenhydramine 50mg IV/PO 1 hour before procedure
- PLUS Famotidine 20mg IV 1 hour before procedure

Renal protection (GFR < 45):
- Hold Metformin 48 hours before and after IV contrast
- IV Normal Saline 1 mL/kg/hr for 3–12 hours pre and post procedure
- N-acetylcysteine 600mg PO BID day before and day of procedure

================================================================================
ANTIMICROBIAL STEWARDSHIP — TOP 10 RESTRICTIONS
================================================================================

RESTRICTED AGENTS (Require Infectious Disease approval or 72h automatic stop):

1. Daptomycin — ID approval required; baseline CPK; weekly CPK monitoring
2. Linezolid — ID approval; maximum 14 days; MAOI interaction screen required
3. Caspofungin — ID approval; baseline LFTs; culture confirmation preferred
4. Colistin/Polymyxin B — ID approval; strict renal dosing; daily Cr monitoring
5. Ertapenem — 72h auto-stop; reassess culture data; avoid for Pseudomonas
6. Meropenem — 72h auto-stop; reserve for MDR GN organisms; ID review
7. Tigecycline — ID approval; high mortality signal in VAP; limited indications
8. Voriconazole — ID approval; TDM required (trough 1–5.5 mcg/mL); LFTs weekly
9. Vancomycin IV — pharmacy-managed AUC dosing; target AUC/MIC 400–600
10. Azithromycin IV — 72h auto-stop; QTc monitoring required; ECG at baseline

De-escalation criteria (review at 48–72 hours):
- Culture results available and organism identified
- Clinical improvement (fever curve, WBC trend, hemodynamic stability)
- Switch to PO equivalent if patient tolerating oral medications
- Document de-escalation rationale in antimicrobial stewardship note
