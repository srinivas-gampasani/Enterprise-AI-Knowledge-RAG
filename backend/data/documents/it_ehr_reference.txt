EPIC EMR QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE
================================================================================

LOGGING IN:
- URL: https://avc-epic.ascension.org (internal network only)
- Two-factor authentication required: Duo Security push to registered mobile device
- Single Sign-On (SSO) via AVC network credentials
- Session timeout: 15 minutes of inactivity (HIPAA requirement)

KEY WORKFLOWS:

Patient Chart Navigation:
- Chart Review: All clinical documentation, results, imaging reports
- Results Review: Labs, vitals, imaging — critical values highlighted in red
- Notes: All provider documentation; cosignature workflows for residents/NPs
- MAR (Medication Administration Record): Medication scanning, administration recording
- Orders: CPOE — all orders placed electronically; paper orders not accepted except downtime

Downtime Procedures:
- Planned downtime: Announced 72 hours in advance via AVC Intranet
- Unplanned downtime: Call IT Helpdesk ext. 4357 immediately
- Downtime paper forms: Located in red "Downtime Binders" at each nursing station
- All paper records scanned and entered into Epic within 4 hours of system restoration

DOWNTIME CRITICAL ACCESS:
- Pharmacy: Pharmacist maintains downtime medication list in Pyxis
- Lab: Critical results called to nursing station directly
- Radiology: Wet reads phoned; portable X-ray for ICU patients

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EPIC IN-BASKET MANAGEMENT:
- Pool messages checked minimum every 2 hours during shift
- Stat messages: Respond within 30 minutes
- Routine messages: Respond within same business day
- Patient portal messages: Respond within 2 business days
- Abnormal result routing: Auto-routed to ordering provider; PA/NP cosigners notified

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SMART PHRASES (COMMONLY USED):
.SEPSIS3 → Inserts Sepsis 3-Hour Bundle Documentation Template
.DISCHARGE → Inserts Discharge Summary Template with all required elements
.FALLRISK → Inserts Morse Fall Scale Assessment Template

CYBERSECURITY & ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY
================================================================================

POLICY ID: AVC-IT-SEC-002 | Effective: October 2024

ACCEPTABLE USE:
- AVC systems and devices are for business purposes
- Limited personal use permitted during break times (no gaming, streaming, shopping)
- All activity on AVC systems is monitored and logged
- No expectation of privacy on AVC-owned equipment or network

PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES:
- Downloading, installing, or running unauthorized software
- Connecting personal storage devices (USB drives, external hard drives) without IT approval
- Sharing passwords or accessing another employee's account (even with their permission)
- Transmitting PHI via personal email, unencrypted messaging apps, or unencrypted email
- Accessing AVC systems via public/unsecured WiFi without VPN

PHI TRANSMISSION STANDARDS:
- Approved secure email: avc-secure@ascension.org gateway (auto-encrypts PHI)
- Approved secure messaging: TigerConnect (enterprise clinical communication platform)
- Fax: Still permitted; use HIPAA-compliant fax cover sheet with confidentiality notice
- SMS/personal messaging apps: PROHIBITED for any PHI

PASSWORD REQUIREMENTS:
- Minimum 12 characters
- Must include: uppercase, lowercase, number, special character
- Cannot reuse last 12 passwords
- Maximum password age: 90 days
- Account lockout: 5 failed attempts (call IT Helpdesk for unlock)

PHISHING & SOCIAL ENGINEERING:
- Report suspicious emails: Forward to phishing@ascension.org
- Do not click links or open attachments in suspicious emails
- IT will NEVER ask for your password via email, phone, or in person
- Annual phishing simulation training; failure requires retraining within 48 hours

TELEHEALTH TECHNICAL STANDARDS
================================================================================

POLICY ID: AVC-TELE-003 | Effective: March 2024

APPROVED TELEHEALTH PLATFORMS:
- Primary: Amwell (integrated with Epic; preferred for scheduled visits)
- Backup: Zoom for Healthcare (HIPAA BAA in place; for urgent or ad-hoc visits)
- NOT approved: FaceTime, Skype, standard Zoom, Google Meet (no BAA)

PROVIDER REQUIREMENTS:
- Complete AVC Telehealth Orientation (3-hour CME credit course in HealthStream)
- Test equipment and internet connection before each session
- Minimum internet speed: 10 Mbps upload/download
- Background: Neutral/professional; patient must see only provider (no other individuals in view)
- Document session in Epic within 2 hours: Include video visit attestation statement

PATIENT CONSENT:
- Verbal consent for telehealth documented in Epic at start of each visit
- Written consent required for: mental health sessions, procedures via telemedicine
- Consent script: "I am documenting your consent to conduct this visit via telehealth..."

TECHNICAL FAILURE PROTOCOL:
If technology fails during a visit:
1. Attempt reconnection once (wait 2 minutes)
2. Switch to telephone if video cannot be restored
3. Document: "Visit converted from telehealth video to telephone due to technical issues"
4. Reschedule if clinical assessment requires video (visual inspection, wound care, etc.)

NETWORK INFRASTRUCTURE & CLINICAL SYSTEMS
================================================================================

NETWORK SEGMENTS:
- Clinical Network (VLAN 10): EMR, medical devices, PACS — isolated, monitored 24/7
- Corporate Network (VLAN 20): Email, internet, administrative systems
- Guest Network (VLAN 30): Patient/visitor WiFi — no access to clinical systems

MEDICAL DEVICE INTEGRATION:
- Patient Monitoring (Philips IntelliVue): Vitals auto-flow to Epic every 15 min
- Infusion Pumps (BD Alaris): DERS (Drug Error Reduction Software) enabled; library updated quarterly
- Glucometers (Roche Accu-Chek): Results transmitted to Epic via Bluetooth within 60 seconds
- Ventilators (Puritan Bennett 980): Manual transcription to Epic; auto-integration roadmap Q3 2025

IMAGING SYSTEMS:
- PACS: Sectra (primary); Merge (legacy archive)
- Diagnostic Workstations: EIZO calibrated monitors; re-calibrated every 6 months
- Critical Result Turnaround: Radiologist to provider notification within 1 hour of read

IT HELPDESK:
- Phone: Extension 4357 (HELP) | External: (316) 268-4357
- Hours: 24/7/365 for clinical systems; 7am–7pm M–F for administrative
- Priority 1 (clinical system down): Response < 15 min
- Priority 2 (degraded performance): Response < 1 hour
- Priority 3 (non-urgent): Response < 4 hours business day
